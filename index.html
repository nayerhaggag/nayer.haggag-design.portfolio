About Me

Hi, I'm Nayer Haggag, a rising junior in high school with a strong interest in engineering and design. I spend much of my free time in my school's design lab, turning ideas into working prototypes — from a solar-powered charger built for an underprivileged community in Egypt, to tools now used in my own classrooms. Each project pushes me to research, prototype, and iterate until something genuinely useful comes out the other end.

Projects

Project Shams

Solar-Powered Charger

Co-founded and built with a friend from my school's design lab, this solar-powered charger channels sunlight directly into a phone. We built and distributed it to Tunis Village, an underprivileged community in Egypt, entirely on our own time.

Independent Project

Solar-Powered Power Bank

An independent redesign of the original charger, built to solve its biggest limitation: needing direct sunlight to work. This version stores solar energy in a battery bank so it can charge a phone anytime.

Class Project

Magnetic Whiteboard Organization Tool

Designed with a classmate for our Design & Engineering class, this laser-cut, magnet-mounted organizer keeps whiteboard markers and a cloth in one place. Six units are now in daily use in teachers' classrooms.

Personal Project

Lawn Mower Cover

Built entirely from materials and tools already in the house, this wooden cover was a birthday gift for my grandfather, turning unused space above his mower into practical, clutter-free storage.
