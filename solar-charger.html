Solar-Powered Charger

Project Shams

The Problem

My friend Aly Abo Ella and I are both makers who spend most of our free time working in our school's design lab. After coming across a similar project online, we decided to build our own solar-powered charger. But once we had the idea, a bigger question followed: what could this actually be used for, and who could it help? Living in Egypt, an underprivileged country, we saw a chance to put the project to real use rather than let it sit as a shelf prototype.

The Approach

We designed a charger that channels power directly from a solar panel through a charge controller and out to a cable, letting a phone charge straight from sunlight. Aly and I worked through every stage together — researching the electronics, modeling the case in CAD, soldering the circuit, and building the wooden housing by hand. Once we had working chargers, we brought them to Tunis Village, a rural community without reliable access to electricity, and distributed them directly to people who needed them.

The Result

Project Shams was never a school assignment or a competition entry — it was something we chose to build and give away entirely on our own time. Seeing a project we designed and built ourselves go on to provide real electricity to people who needed it showed us that a small, student-led idea could have an impact well outside the classroom.
