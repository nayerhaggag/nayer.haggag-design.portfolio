Solar-Powered Power Bank

Independent Project

The Problem

The original Project Shams charger worked, but it had two real limitations: it only worked in direct sunlight, and charging straight from the panel was slower than plugging into an outlet. If clouds rolled in or the sun went down, the charger was useless. I wanted to design a version that could store energy and deliver it on demand instead.

The Approach

Working on my own, I designed a solar power bank that adds an energy storage buffer between the panel and the phone. A 9V solar panel feeds a buck converter that steps the voltage down to a stable 5V, which charges a power bank module responsible for managing safe charging and boosting voltage back up for the phone. Six 18650 lithium-ion cells act as the actual reservoir, storing solar energy throughout the day so it's available anytime, sun or no sun.

The Result

The power bank was designed and assembled as a working prototype. I haven't run formal performance tests on it yet, but it's a direct engineering response to a real limitation I identified in my own earlier project — the kind of iteration that's central to good design.
